Key Notes:
• Co-Leaders: Joe Zawinul (keyboards) and Wayne Shorter (saxophone) remained constants throughout the band's 16-year existence (1970–1986)
• Early Era (1971–1973): Miroslav Vitouš forms the core rhythm section. Frequent drummer/percussionist changes begin
• Mid Era (1974–1977): Introduction of Alphonso Johnson (bass) then Jaco Pastorius. Band reaches commercial and critical peak with "Heavy Weather"
• Late Era (1978–1986): Electronic technology dominance. Peter Erskine provides first rhythm section stability (1978–1982). Final lineup with Victor Bailey/Omar Hakim/Mino Cinélu from 1983–1986
• Studio vs. Live: This table covers only original studio albums. Live albums (8:30, Live in Tokyo) and compilations are excluded
• Guest Appearances: Albums often featured additional guest musicians on specific tracks not listed in core lineup
