Key Points About of Montreal's Discography:

The Founder & Core Identity: of Montreal is fundamentally the project of Kevin Barnes, who founded the band in 1996 in Athens, Georgia, allegedly naming it for a failed romance with a woman from Montreal, Quebec. While the band has featured numerous rotating lineups and collaborators, Kevin Barnes has remained the sole constant, primary songwriter, and creative force throughout the entire 27-year history. Barnes uses all pronouns and has been increasingly open about their identity and emotional struggles in recent years.
Early Period (1997-1999): Athens Formation & Psychedelic Pop Roots: Formed with Derek Almstead and Bryan Poole ("The Late B.P. Helium"), of Montreal began as a whimsical, Beatles-influenced indie pop band with psychedelic and vaudeville elements. Though the band had rotating members, these early albums established the Elephant 6 collective aesthetic: lo-fi charm, wordy lyrics, colorful arrangements. This era feels quaint and innocent compared to later work.
Expansion & Experimentation (2001-2003): Kindercore Years: Signed to Georgia-based Kindercore Records, of Montreal released increasingly ambitious and orchestrated albums. Collaborators like Dottie Alexander (keyboards) and Jamie Huggins (drums) joined during this period. These albums are dense, psychedelic, and quirky—songs with whimsical titles ("Coquelicot Asleep in the Poppies") but sophisticated arrangements. This period ended when Kindercore folded in 2002.
Breakthrough Period (2004-2007): Signing to Polyvinyl / Electronic Turn / Commercial Success: After Kindercore folded, of Montreal signed to Polyvinyl Records and Kevin Barnes began recording almost entirely alone, marking a dramatic shift toward dance/funk-influenced electronic music. Satanic Panic in the Attic (2004) and The Sunlandic Twins (2005) showed a solo artist finding their voice. But Hissing Fauna, Are You the Destroyer? (2007)—recorded while Barnes lived in Norway, separated from their wife—became a critical masterpiece (#72 Billboard) and commercial breakthrough. Emotionally vulnerable and conceptually ambitious, Hissing Fauna remains the band's peak and most-praised album.
Funk & Experimental Phase (2008-2012): Georgie Fruit & Session Musicians: Following Hissing Fauna's success, Barnes further explored funk, R&B, and experimental arrangements through Skeletal Lamping, False Priest (#34 Billboard—their highest chart position), and Paralytic Stalks. This era introduced the character "Georgie Fruit," a dark, hypersexual alter ego representing Barnes' psychological struggles. Paralytic Stalks (2012) marked the first time of Montreal hired session musicians. The era is dense, often indulgent, and critically mixed but artistically adventurous.
Reformation Phase (2013-2016): Return to Live Band / Human Vulnerability: Lousy with Sylvianbriar (2013) marked a major shift: recorded analog to tape with a "live band" feel, returning to songwriting fundamentals and intimacy. Influences shifted to Grateful Dead, Neil Young, and Sylvia Plath. Aureate Gloom (2015) was written after Barnes' separation from their wife of over 10 years, exploring 70s NYC punk-glam (CBGB, Television, Patti Smith). Innocence Reaches (2016) embraced updated glam pop with EDM influences. This period is seen by many as a successful rehabilitation of the band's sound after years of indulgence.
Recent Era (2018-2024): Sustained Experimentation & Rapid Output: Since 2018, of Montreal has maintained a prolific release schedule (annual or near-annual albums). White Is Relic/Irrealis Mood (2018) was synth-heavy dance music inspired by 1980s extended mixes. Ur Fun (2020) was simpler electro-pop. I Feel Safe with You, Trash (2021)—recorded during COVID isolation—is a dense, introspective retrospective fusing multiple eras. Freewave Lucifer f˂ck f˄ck f˃ck (2022) and Lady on the Cusp (2024) continue exploring experimental arrangements and emotional vulnerability. The band's recent output is viewed as uneven but consistently showing creative ambition.
Band Members & Collaborators: Key recurring band members include Dottie Alexander (keyboards, flute, 1998-2013), Derek Almstead (bass/drums, 1996-2003), Jamie Huggins/James Husband (drums, synthesizer, 1998-2010), Clayton Rychlik (drums, keyboards, 2010-present), Bob Parins (bass, pedal steel, 2013-present), JoJo Glidewell (keyboards, 2013-present), Bennett Lewis (guitar, 2013-2015), and Rebecca Cash (vocals, 2013-2014). Producer/collaborators include Jon Brion (False Priest), Kishi Bashi (strings, production), and engineer Drew Vandenberg (Paralytic Stalks, Lousy with Sylvianbriar, Aureate Gloom).
Artistic Evolution & Identity: of Montreal's career is defined by constant stylistic reinvention. The band has moved from Beatles-influenced twee pop → psychedelic pop → electronic funk → glam-punk → synth-dance → introspective vulnerability within a single 27-year span. Kevin Barnes is known for verbose, clever lyrics filled with sexual imagery, emotional rawness, and thesaurus-grade vocabulary. Themes range from whimsical character sketches (early years) to brutally honest autobiographical depression/anxiety/sexuality (Hissing Fauna onwards). The band's visual aesthetic, character development (especially Georgie Fruit), and willingness to experiment with unconventional album formats (10 versions of Skeletal Lamping, analog recording, minimal production) reflect Barnes' commitment to boundary-pushing creativity.
Current Status & Influence: of Montreal remains active with regular touring and recording. Though never achieving mainstream fame, the band has developed a devoted cult following and significant influence on indie pop, psychedelic pop, electronic music, and queer expression in rock music. Barnes' openness about depression, anxiety, sexuality, and gender identity has made of Montreal a touchstone for fans seeking emotionally authentic, sonically adventurous music. The band is considered part of the broader Elephant 6 collective legacy alongside Neutral Milk Hotel, The Apples in Stereo, and Bright Eyes, though of Montreal's trajectory has been the most commercially and artistically ambitious of the group.
